3) Site Structure

This should be a 2–3 page website, not just one long scroll page.

Suggested structure:

Page 1 — Home / About / Highlights

Main landing page with hero section, intro, key metrics, selected experience highlights, selected projects, and a strong first impression.

Page 2 — Journey / Timeline

A storytelling page showing Jayanth’s personal and professional journey from bachelor’s degree to current work, including certifications, internship, full-time roles, graduate school, projects, and ongoing learning.

Page 3 — Contact / Playground / Extras

A page for contact, creative interaction, possibly a mini-game, social links, and a more playful or experimental side of the brand.

If needed, projects can either live on the homepage as featured cards or have their own dedicated section/page.

5) Main UX Principle

This website is not just to show experience.
It is also to showcase web design and product design skills.

That means every major section should feel intentionally designed and interactive.

Important principle:

Each interactive element should have its own unique behavior.
Do not use the same generic expand/collapse interaction everywhere.

For example:

Projects can flip, slide, or animate in a creative way
Certifications can “stamp” or “badge reveal”
Education can unfold like a campus/info card
Experience can open through timeline expansion or visual reveal
Hover states should feel thoughtful and section-specific
6) Special Signature Interaction — Coffee Cup Scroll Animation

This is one of the main creative ideas and should be included.

Concept

Use a coffee mug as a scroll progress indicator.

Behavior
At the top of the page, the mug is full of coffee
As the user scrolls down, the coffee level changes to represent how much of the page remains
The amount of liquid in the mug visually indicates scroll progress

This can be placed:

fixed at the side of the page
in the corner of the screen
or integrated beautifully into the timeline page

This should be subtle and elegant, not distracting.
